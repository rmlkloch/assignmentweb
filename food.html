Food & Cuisine of Sri Lanka

Indulge in the rich and diverse flavors of Sri Lankan cuisine, from spicy curries to sweet treats! Discover the vibrant street food culture, iconic dishes, and unique flavors that define Sri Lanka's culinary heritage.

Kottu Roti

Kottu Roti is a popular street food in Sri Lanka, made by stir-frying chopped pieces of roti (flatbread) with vegetables, meat, and a blend of spices. It's a flavorful, savory dish often enjoyed as a quick and filling meal.

Rice and Curry

Rice and Curry is the heart of Sri Lankan cuisine. The dish includes steamed rice served with an array of flavorful curries made from vegetables, meats, and aromatic spices. It’s a daily staple and varies by region.

Hoppers (Appa)

Hoppers, or 'Appa,' are crispy, bowl-shaped pancakes made from fermented rice flour. They are commonly enjoyed for breakfast or dinner and are often served with a variety of fillings such as eggs, honey, or sambal.
